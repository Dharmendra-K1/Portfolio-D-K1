Teaching Assistant for CFD

As a teaching assistant for Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), I had the incredible opportunity to support students in their journey of understanding the intricate world of fluid dynamics simulations. Guiding them through complex theoretical concepts and practical applications, I witnessed their growth and enthusiasm firsthand. Working with professors Dr. Ramkarn Patne, I facilitated hands-on laboratory sessions, where students honed their CFD skills, analyzed results, and developed innovative solutions to real-world engineering challenges. Witnessing their excitement and progress in mastering CFD techniques was truly rewarding, and it reinforced my passion for sharing knowledge and nurturing the next generation of aspiring engineers in this dynamic field.

Teaching Assistant for ANM

As a teaching assistant for Advanced Numerical Methods, I had the privilege of delving into the fascinating realm of numerical algorithms and their applications. Assisting students in grasping sophisticated numerical techniques, such as finite difference methods, finite volume methods, and iterative solvers, was both challenging and rewarding. Through interactive tutorials and code reviews, I witnessed their problem-solving skills flourish and their understanding of complex mathematical models deepen. It was a gratifying experience to see how these advanced numerical methods empowered students to tackle intricate engineering and scientific problems with confidence and creativity. As a teaching assistant, I felt honored to be part of their academic journey, fostering a passion for numerical analysis and computational simulations.
